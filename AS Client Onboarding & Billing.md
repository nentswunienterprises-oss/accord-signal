**client onboarding and billing should be part of Accord Signal’s operating system**, but not as the main identity.

Accord Signal should have two operating layers:

## 1. Client Delivery OS

This is how Accord Signal serves organizations.

## 2. Internal Business OS

This is how Accord Signal sells, onboards, bills, delivers, reports, and retains clients.

So client onboarding and billing would sit under the **Accord Signal Business OS**, while role architecture, capability mapping, and readiness development sit under the **Accord Signal Delivery OS**.

# Accord Signal Client Onboarding OS

The client onboarding system should move like this:

## 1. Inquiry

A potential client reaches out or is introduced.

Goal:

Understand whether they have a real capability, role, attention, or organizational clarity problem.

Collect:

Company name
Team size
Main problem
Current structure
Urgency
Decision maker
Budget range
Desired outcome

---

## 2. Diagnostic Call

This is not a normal sales call.

It should be framed as a **Capability Clarity Call**.

You are diagnosing:

Are roles unclear?
Is attention scattered?
Are leaders sending mixed signals?
Are people undertrained?
Are systems weak?
Is performance inconsistent?
Is the issue people, structure, signals, or leadership rhythm?

Output:

Initial problem diagnosis
Recommended service path
Whether Accord Signal is a fit

---

## 3. Internal Assessment

After the call, Accord Signal decides what type of client this is.

Possible client categories:

**Role Clarity Client**
They need roles, responsibilities, and expectations defined.

**Capability Development Client**
They need training pathways and skill-building systems.

**Attention Alignment Client**
They need priority systems, communication rhythms, and leadership signals.

**Operating System Client**
They need dashboards, meetings, workflows, reporting, and accountability systems.

**Full Capability Infrastructure Client**
They need the complete Accord Signal OS.

---

## 4. Proposal

The proposal should not feel like a random quotation.

It should be structured around the Accord Signal OS:

**Diagnosis**
What is currently unclear or weak.

**Objective**
What Accord Signal will help build.

**Scope**
What services will be delivered.

**Phases**
How the work will unfold.

**Outputs**
What the client will receive.

**Timeline**
How long it will take.

**Investment**
What it will cost.

**Terms**
Payment structure and conditions.

---

## 5. Agreement and Invoice

Once approved, the client receives:

Service agreement
Invoice
Payment terms
Onboarding form
Kickoff booking link
Document request list

The client should not begin full delivery until the agreement is signed and the required payment is made.

---

## 6. Onboarding Form

The onboarding form collects:

Company details
Main contacts
Team structure
Current roles
Existing documents
Pain points
Current tools
Meeting rhythms
Leadership expectations
Existing training material
Confidentiality requirements
Billing details

---

## 7. Kickoff Session

The kickoff session sets alignment.

Agenda:

Confirm the problem
Confirm the desired outcome
Confirm delivery phases
Confirm communication channels
Confirm deadlines
Confirm client responsibilities
Confirm what success looks like

This is where Accord Signal starts creating clarity from day one.

---

## 8. Delivery Workspace

Each client should get a structured workspace.

Could be in Notion, Google Drive, ClickUp, Trello, or a custom Relief Works system later.

Workspace sections:

Client Overview
Diagnosis
Role Maps
Signal Maps
Capability Maps
Meeting Notes
Documents
Deliverables
Invoices
Progress Tracker
Next Actions

# Accord Signal Billing OS

Billing should depend on the type of service.

## Billing Model 1: Diagnostic Fee

For serious clients, charge for the initial diagnostic.

Example:

**Capability Diagnostic Session**
Once-off fee
Includes discovery call, problem analysis, and written recommendation.

Good for filtering serious clients.

---

## Billing Model 2: Project-Based Fee

Best for defined deliverables.

Examples:

Role Architecture Project
Capability Mapping Project
Onboarding System Build
Team Operating System Design
Leadership Signal System
Role Readiness Framework

Payment structure:

50% upfront
50% before final handover

or

40% upfront
30% midpoint
30% final

---

## Billing Model 3: Monthly Retainer

Best for ongoing support.

Accord Signal becomes the client’s capability infrastructure partner.

Monthly services may include:

Monthly leadership alignment
Role updates
Capability tracking
Team readiness reviews
Operating rhythm support
Dashboard/reporting updates
Training pathway maintenance

Retainer tiers:

**Foundation**
Basic monthly support.

**Operating Partner**
Ongoing capability and role system support.

**Embedded Infrastructure**
Deep involvement with team systems, training, dashboards, and readiness tracking.

---

## Billing Model 4: Implementation + Retainer

This is probably the best model.

First, Accord Signal builds the system.
Then, Accord Signal helps maintain and improve it.

Example:

**Phase 1: Capability Infrastructure Build**
Once-off setup fee.

**Phase 2: Accord Signal Retainer**
Monthly support, review, improvement, and reporting.

This creates both cash flow and long-term client relationships.

---

# Recommended Client Journey

The cleanest journey:

## 1. Capability Clarity Call

Free or paid initial consultation.

## 2. Capability Diagnostic

Paid assessment.

## 3. System Build

Project-based implementation.

## 4. Monthly Operating Support

Retainer.

So the full flow is:

**Call → Diagnostic → Proposal → Invoice → Onboarding → Kickoff → Delivery → Review → Retainer**

# Billing Policy

Accord Signal should be strict here.

Recommended rules:

No project starts without signed agreement.
No full delivery starts without upfront payment.
Invoices are due before kickoff unless otherwise agreed.
Late payments pause delivery.
Scope changes require a revised quote.
Monthly retainers are paid in advance.
Final deliverables are released after final payment.
All client materials remain confidential.

This protects the institution.

Find organizations with unclear roles, weak capability, scattered attention, or poor execution rhythm.

Identify whether the real problem is role clarity, signal quality, attention alignment, capability gap, or readiness weakness.
